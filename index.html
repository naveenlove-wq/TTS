Section 01

Purpose & Scope

This proposal defines a consistent, tier-aware process for transitioning customers out of onboarding and into ongoing success or support. It addresses how readiness is determined, how Customer Success Manager (CSM) assignments are triggered, how handoffs are executed, and what the experience looks like for customers who do not receive a dedicated CSM upon graduation.

The process covers all customer tiers — T1 through T5 — with distinct paths for guided onboarding customers (T1–T3) transitioning to Customer Success, and self-serve SMB customers (T4–T5) transitioning to Support.

Section 03

T1–T3: Transition to Success (TTS)

Guided onboarding customers follow a structured handoff from Implementation to Customer Success. There are two distinct trigger points: CSM assignment occurs at go-live; formal transition occurs at the 2-week mark once readiness criteria are satisfied.

Process Flow

T1–T3 TTS Process Flow

Transition Readiness Criteria

The IM is responsible for assessing readiness. The CSM has explicit authority to block transition during the internal handover call if any required criteria are unmet.

CriteriaClassificationNotes
Customer is live in MoeGo Required Go-live field in Salesforce = True. This also triggers CSM assignment for eligible accounts.
Burndown list complete Required All operational and workflow items resolved. Open bugs or enhancements are not blockers — they pass to CS as managed risk.
No active operational escalation Required Any workflow or day-to-day issue affecting the customer's ability to use MoeGo normally must be resolved prior to transition.
Minimum 2 weeks post go-live Required Stabilization period. CSM assignment happens at go-live; formal transition happens at or after the 2-week mark.

Blocker vs. Passable Risk

Item TypeClassificationHandled By
Workflow or operational issue (prevents normal day-to-day use) Transition blocker IM must resolve before transition proceeds
Open bug or product defect Passable risk Passes to CSM as a tracked open item
Enhancement request or roadmap item Passable risk Passes to CSM as a tracked open item
The CSM has explicit authority — not just the ability — to block a transition during the internal handover call. If the CSM identifies unresolved operational risk, active churn signals, or any item that would prevent successful adoption, they are empowered and expected to refuse the handoff until those items are addressed. Disagreements escalate to CS and Implementation leadership for resolution.
Section 04

T4–T5: Transition to Support

SMB customers completing self-serve onboarding do not receive a dedicated CSM. Their transition is handled through an automated communication sequence triggered by the go-live event in Salesforce, with a proactive health check at 30 days that can route at-risk customers to the support team.

Process Flow

T4–T5 Transition to Support Process Flow

Automated Email Sequence

1

Go-live trigger fires (Salesforce)

Automated go-live mechanism marks the customer live in Salesforce, triggering a Zapier workflow with a 1-week delay before the first customer-facing email sends.

2

1-week monitoring window

Support escalation activity is monitored during the first 7 days. If an active escalation exists at the 7-day mark, the go-live email is held and flagged for review rather than sending automatically.

3

Go-live celebration email

Congratulations message acknowledging that the customer is live and seeing their first appointments in MoeGo. Includes self-serve resource links for continued adoption: memberships, marketing integrations, leads management, campaign tracking, reporting features, and Wiki guidance for independent setup.

4

30-day health check email

Automated email at the 30-day mark asking the customer to share their experience with MoeGo so far. Responses are routed based on sentiment threshold (threshold TBD — see Section 06).

✓

Transition to Support complete

Negative responses trigger proactive outreach from the support team to schedule a call and address any issues. Positive or neutral responses continue on standard self-serve support. Health signal is recorded regardless of outcome.

The negative response threshold (NPS score range, binary rating, etc.), Zapier conditional logic for the escalation hold, and specific email copy are open items listed in Section 06.
Section 05

CSM Exception Request Process

This process allows a guided onboarding customer below the ARR threshold to be assigned a CSM when a strategic justification exists. It is a formal process requiring CS leadership approval — not an informal workaround. Initiated by the AE or IM most familiar with the account.

StepOwnerAction
1. Identify need AE or IM AE or IM identifies a below-threshold customer with a strategic case for CS support.
2. Submit request AE or IM Submits CSM exception request form. Form includes a reason category (list TBD — see Section 06) and a required narrative justification field. Supporting context such as growth projections or strategic notes can be included.
3. Review CS Leadership CS leadership reviews the request against the narrative and reason category. Decision communicated within agreed SLA (SLA TBD — see Section 06).
4. Outcome CS Leadership Approved: CSM assigned, TTS process proceeds normally. Denied: requesting party notified with rationale; customer continues on standard support path.
