01 — Overview

Purpose and scope

This document defines MoeGo's process for moving customers out of onboarding and into their post-onboarding relationship — either with a dedicated Customer Success Manager (Transition to Success) or into a self-serve support model (Transition to Support). It covers all customer tiers, defines readiness criteria, documents the handoff mechanics, and establishes the exception process for below-threshold customers who may need CS coverage.

Two distinct paths. T1–T3 customers transition to a CSM (Transition to Success). T4–T5 customers transition to support resources (Transition to Support). The process, timing, and tooling differ meaningfully between the two.

03 — Transition to Success

TTS process: T1–T3

There are two distinct moments in the TTS process. CSM assignment is triggered at go-live. Formal handoff is triggered after the 2-week post-live period, once the customer has cleared all readiness criteria. These are separate events.

Readiness criteria

Required to proceed

  • Customer is confirmed live in MoeGo
  • Burndown list fully resolved
  • Minimum 2 weeks post-go-live elapsed
  • No active escalations
  • CSM has been briefed and formally accepts the transition

Does not block transition

  • Open enhancement requests
  • Open bugs tracked by product or engineering
  • Feature requests under backlog review
  • Non-critical open support tickets
Blocker definition. Any item that is workflow-related or affects day-to-day operations is a hard blocker and must be resolved before transition. Open bugs, enhancements, and non-operational items pass to the CSM as documented managed risk.

Process flow

Handoff components

  1. TTS form completed by the IM prior to the internal handover call. Documents account health, open items, key contacts, and go-live summary.
  2. Internal handover call between IM and CSM. The CSM reviews the account, confirms no active blockers, and formally accepts the transition. The CSM has the authority — and responsibility — to block the transition if readiness criteria are not met. This is not advisory.
  3. Warm handoff to customer introduces the CSM, clarifies what being "in success" means operationally versus onboarding, and sets expectations for the CS relationship going forward.
CSM authority. The CSM can and should block a transition on the internal handover call if the customer does not meet readiness criteria. If the IM and CSM disagree on readiness, the item escalates to CS leadership and Implementation leadership for resolution.
04 — Exception Request

CSM exception request process

Customers below the $12K ARR threshold are not automatically assigned a CSM. This process allows an AE or IM to formally request CS coverage for an exception case. All requests are reviewed and approved by CS leadership.

Exception request flow

Request form fields

05 — Transition to Support

Transition to Support: T4–T5

SMB and self-serve customers do not receive a dedicated CSM. Post-onboarding is handled through an automated sequence triggered by go-live in Salesforce, with support as the primary resource for help and structured touchpoints to surface dissatisfaction early.

Automated email sequence

Go-live trigger

Go-live confirmation

Sent 1 week after the live trigger fires in Salesforce (via Zapier), contingent on no active escalations. Celebrates the milestone and provides links to the wiki and feature adoption resources: memberships, marketing integrations, leads management, campaign tracking, and reporting.

→
30 days post-live

Health check

Sentiment check at 30 days. Negative responses automatically route to a support outreach to schedule a call and address open issues. Positive responses continue on the self-serve adoption path.

Transition to Support flow

Go-live email trigger logic

Email sends when all are true

  • Customer is marked live in Salesforce
  • 1 full week has elapsed since go-live
  • No active critical support escalations

If escalation is active at day 7

  • Email is held and not sent
  • Account is flagged for support review
  • Support team follows up directly
Open item: support outreach SLA. A negative 30-day health check response without a defined support outreach SLA is an unmanaged churn risk. The SLA for support follow-up on negative responses should be defined before this process goes live.
