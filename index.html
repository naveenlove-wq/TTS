01 — Overview

Purpose and scope

This document defines MoeGo's process for moving customers out of onboarding and into their post-onboarding relationship — either with a dedicated Customer Success Manager (Transition to Success) or into a self-serve support model (Transition to Support). It covers all customer tiers, defines readiness criteria, documents handoff mechanics, and establishes the exception process for below-threshold customers who may need CS coverage.

Two distinct paths. T1–T3 customers transition to a CSM (Transition to Success). T4–T5 customers transition to support resources (Transition to Support). The process, timing, and tooling differ meaningfully between the two.

03 — Transition to Success

TTS process: T1–T3

T1 Enterprise: CSM assigned at kickoff

T1 accounts receive a dedicated CSM before onboarding begins. The CSM is identified and assigned during pre-kickoff coordination between Implementation and CS leadership, and is introduced to the customer at or before the kickoff call. The formal TTS handoff still applies at go-live, but the CSM relationship is established from day one.

T2/T3 Mid-Market: weekly CS and Implementation assignment review

For T2 and T3 accounts, CSM assignment is driven by a weekly review cadence between CS leadership and Implementation. As part of the Salesforce buildout, a report will surface all live accounts at the parent level that have not yet been assigned a CSM. CS leadership reviews this list each week, assigns a CSM where the account qualifies, and coordinates with Implementation on any accounts that need additional context before the handoff call is scheduled.

Why a weekly review, not an automatic trigger. The weekly cadence gives CS leadership visibility to assign the right CSM, surface capacity constraints, and flag accounts that need a closer look. This is enabled by the Salesforce reporting infrastructure currently in development — once that reporting is live, this becomes a standard weekly operational task.

Readiness criteria

Required to proceed

  • Customer is confirmed live in MoeGo
  • Burndown list fully resolved
  • Minimum 2 weeks post-go-live elapsed
  • No active escalations
  • CSM has been briefed and formally accepts the transition

Does not block transition

  • Open enhancement requests
  • Open bugs tracked by product or engineering
  • Feature requests under backlog review
  • Non-critical open support tickets
Blocker definition. Any item that is workflow-related or affects day-to-day operations is a hard blocker and must be resolved before transition. Open bugs, enhancements, and non-operational items pass to the CSM as documented managed risk.

TTS process flow (T2/T3)

Handoff components

  1. TTS form completed by the IM prior to the internal handover call. Documents account health, open items, key contacts, and go-live summary.
  2. Internal handover call between IM and CSM. The CSM reviews the account, confirms no active blockers, and formally accepts the transition. The CSM has the authority — and responsibility — to block the transition if readiness criteria are not met. This is not advisory.
  3. Warm handoff to customer introduces the CSM, clarifies what being "in success" means operationally versus onboarding, and sets expectations for the CS relationship going forward.
CSM authority. The CSM can and should block a transition on the internal handover call if the customer does not meet readiness criteria. If the IM and CSM disagree on readiness, the item escalates to CS leadership and Implementation leadership for resolution.
04 — Exception Request

CSM exception request process

Customers below the $12K ARR threshold are not automatically assigned a CSM. This process allows an AE or IM to formally request CS coverage for an exception case. All requests are reviewed and approved by CS leadership.

Exception request flow

Request form fields

FieldRequiredNotes
Customer name and account ID Required
Current ARR Required Must be below the $12K threshold to qualify for exception review
Reason for request Required Select from defined reason list — to be finalized (see open items)
Narrative justification Required Free-text. Always available alongside the reason list
Requestor name and role Required AE or IM
Form delivery options — pending assessment. The exception request can be built as a Slack workflow, a Google Form, or a Salesforce internal case. Each option carries different tradeoffs in terms of efficacy, implementation lift, and long-term scalability. Priority is the Salesforce case route — keeping the process inside SF reduces tool proliferation and aligns with the broader SF buildout already underway. The three options should be formally assessed before committing to a build.
05 — Transition to Support

Transition to Support: T4–T5

SMB customers do not receive a dedicated CSM. The go-live email marks the formal transition to support — from that point, the customer is "in support." The 30-day check-in is a health signal and early churn indicator that occurs while the customer is already in support, not part of the transition gate itself.

Timeline

Go-live → 2-week monitoring window → go-live email (transition complete) → 30 days post-transition → health check email. The health check lands approximately 6 weeks after the customer goes live, giving meaningful time for the customer to operate independently before sentiment is assessed.

Automated email sequence

2 weeks post-live — this is the transition

Go-live confirmation email

Sent 2 weeks after the live trigger fires in Salesforce (via Zapier), contingent on no active escalations or risk signals. Marks the formal transition to support. Provides wiki links and feature adoption resources: memberships, marketing integrations, leads management, campaign tracking, and reporting.

→
30 days post-transition — in-support risk signal

Health check email

Sent 30 days after the go-live email, not 30 days after go-live. Customer is already in support at this point. Negative responses route to support outreach to schedule a call. Positive responses continue on the self-serve adoption path.

Framing note. The go-live email is the handoff. The 30-day check-in is a health signal while the customer is already in support. These are distinct touchpoints with different owners and purposes.

Transition to Support flow

Open item: escalation and risk signal definition. The 2-week gate requires a clear, agreed-upon definition of what constitutes an active escalation or risk signal for T4/T5 accounts. Candidates include open cases in Intercom above a threshold, users not yet active in SF/MIS, future appointments not booked, and MoeGo Pay not utilized. Defining this requires cross-functional input from Support, CS, Implementation, and the Salesforce engineer. See open items section.
